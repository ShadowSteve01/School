Welcome to the Models


These are some of the models I have created since the begining of lockdown,
most of the models I had rendered where from my Blender exports when I was trying to lean how to use the program.
Each render was a different experiment including modelling techniques, texturing adjustments, camera work and
basing the model off a more realistic and accurate model.

In the future I plan to make even more models to expand my skills of modelling,
when I make more models I will be trying to expand out from just Daleks and try working
on enviroments and artifacts such as buildings, cars, small discarded peices of trash
and many more. I do use different techniques for modelling as well such as modelling from blueprints,
box modelling, and somtimes extrusion modelling. I find both Extrustion and Box modelling are my,
more prefered method of modelling and I have more skill in working in those areas, plus it's easier
to use those techniques compared to spending lots of time searching for blueprints to go off.
So I look forward to the future developments of my modelling and I can't wait to reveal any developments
on this site.

On the right is an image of a vechile I created for a modelling task/ competition in University, I call the
finished product the Sandcrawler. When I designed the Sandcrawler I thought of making some form of vechile in
a wasteland apocalypse or even to make some form of hover bike, I also liked the idea of making some form of motor cycle
so I started making some designs by bashing a few shapes together. After finishing the basic geometry of the object I then
started to extrude and combine the shapes together and made the basic form of the Sandcrawler. Then I went back over
the model with boolean techniques and made some wires to cover some empty spaces using the Lattice tool over cylinders.
Then I finished the model and later added a few extra details to the design including some scratches and some small references,
such as a Captain America's sheild on the side of the machine. I would have liked to add some form of sculpting to the model
and I would have liked to UV it but sadly the UV mesh was very big and would have taken a large portion of time to create.
Overall I enjoyed the project and I think it came out with a decent result.
